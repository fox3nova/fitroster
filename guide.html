Plans and exercises

Build your training language.

FitRoster treats a workout menu as a reusable training structure. You can adjust exercise names freely, add your own exercises, and keep built-in names localized while custom names stay exactly as you typed them.

  • Rename exercise rows to match your gym, coaching vocabulary, or equipment labels.
  • Create custom exercises and organize them into reusable templates or free-training menus.
  • Keep planned items separate from completed training records so history stays clean.

Coach and trainee

Keep coaching tied to the plan.

The coach flow separates planning from returned records. A coach can prepare menus, send a plan snapshot, review a trainee's calendar and submissions, then refine the next block without losing the original training context.

  • Send shared or trainee-specific plans from the coach training surface.
  • The trainee accepts a received plan, trains from a local copy, and returns completed progress.
  • Coach records stay focused on schedules, returned sessions, goals, and review work.

Records and analysis

Read progress without guessing.

FitRoster connects completed sessions with calendar history, interval KPIs, body data, goals, and muscle distribution. Analysis should explain what changed, not just display numbers.

  • Use records to check what was actually completed by day, week, or month.
  • Use muscle distribution to see trained and under-trained areas visually.
  • Combine goals and InBody context with workout history before changing the next plan.

Operational details

Use FitRoster as a durable training system.

Settings and language

Keep language, units, role startup, backup, diagnostics, and support links in Settings. Built-in exercise names follow the app language.

Analysis interpretation

Use analysis to compare training load, consistency, goals, and body data over a period instead of judging a single workout alone.

Sync and backup

Use iCloud sync for coach-trainee workflows when available, and keep FitRoster backup/export files for release-grade portability.
